  Cats communicate using meows, purrs, hisses, and body language. Their sounds
  and movements express needs, emotions, and social signals, forming a subtle,
  effective communication system.
---

Cats don’t "speak" in a language like humans do, but they communicate in several
clear and expressive ways:

**1. Meowing** The classic cat sound. Interestingly, adult cats mostly meow to
communicate with humans, not other cats. A short, soft meow might be a greeting,
while a long or loud one could mean hunger, annoyance, or a demand for
attention.

**2. Purring** That gentle vibrating sound usually means contentment, like when
they’re relaxed or being petted. But cats can also purr when they’re stressed or
in pain, possibly as a way to calm themselves.

**3. Hissing and growling** These are warning sounds. A hiss says "back off,"
while a growl signals fear, anger, or that the cat feels threatened.

**4. Chirps and trills** These are cute, bird-like sounds cats often use to
greet their owners or get attention. Mother cats also use them to communicate
with kittens.

**5. Body language (their "silent speech")** Cats rely heavily on physical cues:

- Tail up = friendly, confident
- Tail puffed = scared or defensive
- Slow blinking = trust and affection
- Ears back = irritation or fear

So, a cat "speaks" through a mix of sounds and body signals. If you pay
attention to both, you can usually tell exactly what it’s trying to say.
